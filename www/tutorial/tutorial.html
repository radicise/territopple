Quick Start

Welcome to Territopple!

This short guide will teach you everything you need to know to start playing the eponymous territory toppling game. If you ever need a quick refresher on something, don't forget to check out the Reference!

Basic Layout:

Territopple is played on a grid of tiles. Each tile will have one or more pieces on it, either belonging to a team or unclaimed.

Turns:

Players take turns in order. On their turn, each player must place a piece onto either a neutral space, or a space owned by their team.
If a player has no valid moves, they lose. Play continues until one team owns all the tiles.

Toppling:

If the number of pieces on a tile exceeds the number of neighbors that tile has, the tile will topple. Toppling resets a tile to having one piece, and adds a piece to each of its neighbors. This can cause chain reactions.
Any tile that is toppled onto will become owned by the team that just played their move.
Toppling is the only way to claim tiles that are already owned by other teams.

Huge Games (Coming Soon)

These are special games made for many people to join in on without obligation.
All players are on the same team playing against an AI opponent.
Players gain moves at fixed intervals. Whenever a player makes a move, the AI immediately makes a move as well.
There are a limited number of huge games, and some may be limited time events.
For more details about huge games, check with the server operator.

Tactics Showcase

This section is a showcase of some common tactics that have emerged in the course of multiple games of Territopple. While they are useful, you may prefer to discover them yourself.

Doubling Up

By creating a setup where there is a tile one piece away from being volatile, and multiple adjacent volatile pieces in the same volatile chain, a one-way path for toppling is made.
This allows you to safely set up topples onto your opponents without them being able to retaliate as easily.
